Design Basics Zine

An experimental zine project that explores layout design and typographic expression.

Objective:

Create and design a zine for incoming design major students and/or people who are new to typography and graphic design. Be creative with layout designs and typographic elements. Create a cohesive and unifying design theme that makes use of both images and text.

Ideation:

For my project I wanted to create a zine geared to incoming design majors and people new to graphic design. That means that my content aimed to introduce others to design methods, ideologies, principles, etc.

My layouts were 6 x 7 inches which is a standard booklet size. I chose this size as a way to cleanly organize informational content. My zine featured informational pages, visually experimental designs, and designer interviews.

To begin the ideation phase I created multiple single page and spread layouts. This was meant to experiment with interesting ways to display imagery and textual content.

Digital Drafts:

After doing several pages of brainstorming sketches, I chose my strongest layouts and jumped into Adobe InDesign.

For my zine all of the textual content was taken from pre-written external sources, along with the image. All of this content was used purely for educational purposes.

During this phase I refined and adjusted the content to my sketches. I discovered what designs worked for the content, and which designs could be better.

I also outlined a solid grid system to maintain consistency throughout my pages.

Design Style:

As the main typeface, I chose Futura. It’s used for all body text and in some headers. I experimented with capitals and various type styles to create visual interest and emphasis. Frutiger was also used for some headers to differentiate content.

The driving color of the zine is a bold turquoise color. This color is impactful without being overwhelming or distracting.
