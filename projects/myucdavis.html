myUCdavis Desktop Redesign

A website redesign aimed to fit the needs of students based off of user research.

Context:

REDESIGN - myUCdavis
On paper, myUCdavis is a platform that can be used as a ‚Äúbase‚Äù to navigate to other websites UC Davis students use daily (Canvas, Schedule Builder, etc.) Despite this, it is rarely used by students across the board.

Research Goals:

  • Learn about why UC Davis students use myUCdavis.
  • Understand students‚Äô experience with using myUCdavis
  • Gain insight in common interests, issues, and preferences with students

Research Results:

  • Primarily used to navigate to other sites
  • Some difficulties navigating the website/finding what you need
  • 20% of our respondents knew about the customization and utilize it
  • Several students were looking for similar things; such as academic records, finances, etc
  • Researched other school websites navigation which influenced our final design

Ideation:

To begin the design process each member participating in creating design sketches to get our ideas down on paper.

We then combined our brainstorms together and identified common themes between our ideas which were: sidebar with links, uniformity with tile sizes and orientations, drop down menus, and reduction of visual content on main page.

I also went ahead and did a user flow chart to understand how a user would search for content.

I found that the two most used functions were the tiles and the top bar navigation meaning that those two features¬†needed to be designed in a way that was intuitive, simple, and easy to access.

Otherwise, users may leave the site due to frustration or confusion.

Prototyping: Lo-fi Sketches

To begin the prototyping, we started with lofi-sketches. These were created based off the the idea we had combined from our brainstorms. Erika created the basic layout for the desktop and I created the stylistic direction for our designs.

Based off of our user research, users wanted an interface that was clutter-free and easily navigatable.

Because of that, the team and I kept clearity in mind when coming up with out inital designs.

Prototyping: Mid-fi

For our mid-fi prototype we¬†took ideas from our lo-fi sketches to start working on our final design.¬†We established margins between content,¬†standardized the widths and heights of tiles,¬†began working on a standardized layout of tiles, and¬†began implementing the customize view.

We then made further developments to then conduct user testing on. We received helpful insight from our participants which helped us refine our final design.

Final Desktop Design:

The final redesign of myUCdavis aimed to be a user-friendly and intuitive site that encouraged users to comeback.

The minimalistic design was implemented to reduce clutter and confusion. However, the minimal design still contains the necessary tools for UC Davis students.

Maintaining the user's ability to customize the layout of their hub was also essential to making sure content was relevant to the user.

Improvements & Next Steps:

Due to time constraint, my team and I were unable to develop certain aspects of our design. Here are some improvements we would like to make:

First off, we want to flesh out suggestions from user testing that we did not get to.

This includes improving widget functionality and variety, having sidebar pop-ups expand to the side and expand by just hovering over it, and movability with the ordering of widgets.

Secondly, we want to look at gathering more data from users and creating a mobile version of myUCdavis.
